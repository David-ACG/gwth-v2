# Review Notes — Round 1: Student Perspective Analysis

## Perspective 1: Mid-Career Professional (40s, marketing manager, not technical)

**What works:**
- The "wake-up call" framing is compelling — mirrors what they're hearing from colleagues
- "No coding required" is the single most important message for this audience
- The pricing section is clear and fair
- Three-audience breakdown helps them self-identify immediately

**What needs work:**
- The doom-and-gloom opening goes on too long before offering hope. This person is already anxious — they need reassurance sooner.
- "50% of entry-level white-collar jobs" stat may make them feel it's already too late for them, since they're mid-career. Need to reframe: mid-career people have ADVANTAGES (domain expertise, networks, judgment)
- The METR statistics and "doubling every 7 months" are too technical/abstract. Replace with concrete "before/after" examples they can relate to (e.g., "A marketing manager who used to spend 3 days on competitive analysis now does it in 2 hours")
- Missing: What does a typical week look like? 5 hours sounds manageable but they need to visualise fitting it into their schedule
- Missing: Social proof. Testimonials, case studies, or even hypothetical student journeys would strengthen credibility

## Perspective 2: Recent Graduate (22, business degree, anxious about job market)

**What works:**
- The urgency resonates — they FEEL this already
- "Build a portfolio" message is exactly what they need to hear
- Month 2's industry specialisations feel relevant (they want to stand out)

**What needs work:**
- The pricing might feel steep for someone just starting out. Need to emphasise the ROI — "less than a Netflix subscription after 3 months" and "builds skills worth thousands in potential salary"
- Too much emphasis on "your job is under threat." For a recent grad, the opportunity framing is more motivating: "get hired faster, stand out from every other applicant"
- The "Family AI Bot" capstone sounds very... domestic. Recent grads want to build portfolio pieces that impress employers. Mention that Month 2 and 3 capstones ARE impressive professional portfolio pieces (customer support chatbot, AI readiness assessment tool)
- Missing: How does this compare to a bootcamp or university course? Why choose GWTH over a £10K coding bootcamp?
- Missing: LinkedIn integration and employer-facing certification need more emphasis

## Perspective 3: Small Business Owner (35, runs a 10-person agency, wants to stay competitive)

**What works:**
- "Replace a £20/month subscription" (Month 2) directly speaks to their cost-consciousness
- The three-month progression from personal to team to enterprise makes sense for growing a business
- Independence and no-bias tool recommendations are hugely valuable — they don't trust vendor-funded education
- "Become the AI person" resonates even as a business owner — they want their TEAM to be AI-literate

**What needs work:**
- Too individual-focused. A business owner wants to know: can I put my whole team through this? Is there a team plan?
- The monthly pricing needs a team/enterprise option or at least a "we're working on it" mention
- Missing: Concrete ROI examples for small businesses — "Agency X automated their reporting, saving 15 hours/week across the team"
- The "no discount codes" policy is principled but may feel inflexible to someone wanting to buy 10 seats
- They'd want to know about the time commitment per employee, not just per student

## Perspective 4: Parent (45, concerned about their teenager's future)

**What works:**
- "Rethink what you're telling your kids" section from Schumer resonates deeply
- No-coding-required means their kid doesn't need to "learn to code" first
- The progression from fun projects to professional portfolio appeals to parents' desire for structured education

**What needs work:**
- The document is too adult/career-focused. A parent buying this for their 16-year-old needs to know it's appropriate and engaging for teens
- Month 1's Family AI Bot is actually perfect for families — but the doc doesn't highlight this enough
- Missing: Age recommendation. Is this suitable for 14+? 16+?
- Missing: How does this complement school/university education rather than replace it?
- The doom framing ("50% of jobs eliminated") would terrify a parent. Needs a stronger "your child will be prepared" message

## Perspective 5: Employer/HR Director (50, considering for team training)

**What works:**
- "Companies investing in AI training retain 34% more staff" — this is exactly the stat they need for board approval
- Month 3's enterprise focus and governance frameworks show this isn't just a toy
- The practical, project-based approach appeals to L&D professionals tired of theory-heavy courses

**What needs work:**
- No enterprise pricing or team licensing information
- No mention of progress tracking for managers (can they see how their team is doing?)
- Missing: Implementation plan for rolling this out across a department
- Missing: Compliance and data handling — where is student data stored? GDPR compliance?
- The dynamic scoring system needs more explanation for HR — they'll be selling this to internal stakeholders
- They'd want white-label or co-branding options mentioned, even if not available yet

---

## Cross-Cutting Issues

1. **Tone balance:** The opening is too heavy on fear. Needs rebalancing: acknowledge the reality, but lead more quickly to empowerment.

2. **Missing social proof:** No testimonials, case studies, or numbers. Even "Join X students already learning" or hypothetical student journeys would help.

3. **The "no code" message could be stronger:** This is GWTH's #1 differentiator from every coding bootcamp. Should be a headline, not buried in a bullet list.

4. **5 hours/week not emphasised enough:** This fits into a lunch break + two evenings. Huge selling point vs bootcamps that demand 40+ hours/week.

5. **Free labs not emphasised enough:** This is the on-ramp. Should be a clear CTA: "Try our free labs and see for yourself."

6. **The Schumer source material has a powerful line about dreams getting closer.** The doc barely uses this. The "your dreams just got a lot closer" framing — AI as a superpower that ENABLES things you couldn't do before — is incredibly motivating and underused.

7. **GWTH's 25 years of solution architecture experience** is barely mentioned. This credibility marker differentiates from random YouTube courses.

8. **The scoring system (Curiosity Index, Consistency Score, etc.)** is unique and gamified — should be mentioned as an engagement feature.
