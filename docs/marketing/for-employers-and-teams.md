## The Business Case for AI Training

AI adoption is spreading four times faster than desktop internet did. That is not a projection. It is already happening.

And yet the data tells a sobering story: only 5% of companies generate meaningful value from AI at scale (BCG). Not because the technology doesn't work — it demonstrably does. Because 70% of digital transformations fail due to people and process, not technology.

The gap is not tools. It is skills.

Companies that invest in AI training retain 34% more staff (Anthropic Economic Index). Early AI adopters are growing revenue 1.5 times faster than their peers (OpenAI). And 77% of enterprise AI usage is automation — the kind of work that every employee, not just your engineering team, can learn to do.

The companies that will lead in three years are not the ones buying the most AI tools today. They are the ones whose people know how to use them.

### The real risk is inaction

Most organisations are stuck in one of two places:

1. **Pilot purgatory.** A few enthusiasts are experimenting, but there is no structured skill-building, no governance framework, and no path from individual experiments to organisational capability.
2. **Vendor dependency.** They have purchased enterprise AI tools but adoption is low because employees were never taught how to think with AI — only how to click buttons in a specific product.

Both paths lead to the same outcome: money spent, little returned. The missing piece is systematic, vendor-neutral AI education that turns your entire workforce into capable AI users.
