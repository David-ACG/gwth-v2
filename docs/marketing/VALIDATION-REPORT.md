## Issues Found and Fixed

### Round 1 — Student Perspective Review (see REVIEW-NOTES-round1.md)

Key changes made to the main document:
1. Moved empowerment messaging earlier — less doom before offering hope
2. Added mid-career professional advantages section
3. Strengthened "no coding" as headline-level message
4. Added "5 hours a week" prominence
5. Added "free labs" as clear CTA
6. Added "your dreams just got a lot closer" framing from Schumer
7. Added 25 years experience mention
8. Added scoring system details (Curiosity Index, etc.)
9. Added small business owner as explicit audience

### Round 2 — Factual Accuracy Check

1. **FIXED: Family AI Bot description in landing page** was inaccurate ("answers questions, manages schedules"). Corrected to match syllabus: "Record your weekly family meeting. AI transcribes it, extracts tasks, books calendar events, creates a meal plan, and generates a shopping list."

2. **FIXED: Social media Tweet #3** presented a hypothetical as fact ("A small business owner replaced three subscriptions"). Changed to "Imagine replacing..." framing since we don't have real testimonials yet.

3. **FIXED: Missing "Agentic Mindset" concept** — added dedicated section to main document explaining the prompt->evaluate->improve->repeat cycle.

4. **FIXED: Missing "soft and hard skills" mention** — added to Applied AI section of main document.

### Round 3 — Founder Feedback (15 points — 2026-02-19)

All 15 feedback points applied across all 6 documents:

1. **AI versatility beyond building** — All docs now mention research, writing, data analysis, content creation, ideation, and automation alongside building. Previously over-indexed on "building" as the primary capability.

2. **Removed "best tutor for $20/month" framing** — Replaced with explanation of why self-teaching with AI alone won't work: you don't know what you don't know, AI training data comes from outdated courses, can't guarantee up-to-date methods. Applied in main doc.

3. **Science-backed 1hr/day learning** — All docs now cite Anders Ericsson's deliberate practice research (1hr/day effective cap), the spacing effect (200+ studies), Ebbinghaus forgetting curve (80% forgotten in 24hrs without review), and Michael Simmons' 5-Hour Rule (Gates, Buffett, Musk). Main doc has full detail; other docs reference appropriately.

4. **94 projects counted and cited** — All docs now state "94 projects" (24 in Month 1, 35 in Month 2, 35 in Month 3) instead of "3 capstones." Mention that more are being added constantly.

5. **Urgency — avalanche of new models** — All docs now convey that new models and services launch weekly, GWTH is the only course designed to keep pace, and the window for being early is closing.

6. **Course never ends / always up to date** — Subheadlines and key descriptions now include "Updated every day so your skills never go stale" and "The course never ends — and neither does your advantage."

7. **Video walkthroughs prominently featured** — Every doc now clearly states that each lesson ends with a project AND a video walkthrough where the instructor takes the student through it step by step. Previously this was barely mentioned.

8. **"Updated every day" not "monthly"** — All instances of "updated monthly" changed to "updated every day" across all 6 documents.

9. **AI-first building experience from day one** — All relevant docs now mention "building with AI tools from day one, from the first release of Cursor to the first release of Claude Code" alongside the 25 years of enterprise experience.

10. **Gentler Section 6 on landing page** — Completely rewritten. Now says "We will hold your hand every step of the way" with emphasis on video walkthroughs showing exactly what to do. No longer leads with fear.

11. **No $112.50 total mentioned** — Removed all instances of the total course cost calculation. Only monthly figures ($37.50/mo, $7.50/mo) are used.

12. **No yearly price — free to leave** — All docs with pricing now include: "No yearly price. We believe the course is good enough that you're free to leave at any point. No lock-in, no penalty."

13. **No bulk discounts / enterprise management tools** — Employer doc updated: same per-person price for teams (already lowest possible), but teams get an enterprise admin dashboard with progress tracking, completion rates, certification status, and ability to designate optional lessons.

14. **Don't show full curriculum to unregistered users** — Removed "Read the full curriculum" CTAs. Replaced with Tech Radar and free labs CTAs. Landing page secondary CTA changed from "View the full curriculum" to "Browse the Tech Radar."

15. **Tech Radar + labs visible to all users** — Free labs and Tech Radar are now positioned as accessible to everyone (registered and unregistered) across all docs.

2. **Age appropriateness**: Main doc mentions teenagers. May want to clarify minimum age (16?) when the course launches.

3. **The Agentic Mindset principle** is only explicitly mentioned in the main document. Consider weaving this concept into the landing page and email sequence as well, since it's a key differentiator from other AI courses.

4. **Enterprise admin dashboard**: Described in employer doc but not yet built. Ensure feature is ready before employer page goes live.

5. **Tech Radar page**: Referenced as `/tech-radar` in multiple docs. Ensure this URL exists and is publicly accessible before launch.
