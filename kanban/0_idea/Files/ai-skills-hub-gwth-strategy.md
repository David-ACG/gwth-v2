## 1. The UK AI Skills Hub — What It Is

On 28 January 2026, the UK Government announced a major expansion of its **AI Skills Boost** programme, aiming to upskill **10 million workers** with foundational AI skills by 2030. Every UK adult is eligible for free, benchmarked AI courses through the AI Skills Hub (aiskillshub.org.uk).

Key facts:
- Government calls it the biggest targeted training programme since the Open University
- Targets nearly a third of the UK workforce, including 2 million SME employees
- Only 21% of UK workers feel confident using AI at work
- Only 1 in 6 UK businesses using AI as of mid-2025
- Micro businesses 45% less likely to adopt AI than large businesses
- £27 million TechLocal funding for AI degrees, graduate traineeships, and local tech jobs
- New AI and the Future of Work Unit launched to monitor labour market impacts

### Founding Partners
Accenture, Amazon, Barclays, BT, Google, IBM, Intuit, Microsoft, Sage, SAS, Salesforce

### New Partners (Jan 2026)
British Chambers of Commerce, Cisco, Cognizant, CBI, DfE, DWP, FSB, IOD, LGA, Multiverse, NHS, Pax8, techUK

### How It Works
- The Hub is an **online directory/signposting service** — it aggregates and links to courses hosted on providers' own sites
- Courses are curated by PwC based on criteria covering quality, relevance, and suitability of format
- PwC won a **£4.2 million contract** to build and operate the Hub
- The Hub is aligned to the Innovate UK BridgeAI programme
- Initially focused on four sectors: Agriculture/Food, Construction, Creative Industries, Transport/Logistics/Warehousing

### Two Tiers
1. **AI Skills Hub (broad)** — 600+ courses from various providers, mix of free and paid
2. **AI Skills Boost (badged)** — Only 14 courses, all from big US tech companies, assessed against Skills England's AI foundation skills benchmark. Completers receive a government-backed virtual AI foundations badge

## 2. The Hub's Problems — Why It's Been Savaged

The Hub has received devastating criticism from multiple sources since launch. This is well-documented and creates a significant opportunity for GWTH.ai.

### Quality & Content Issues
- 595 courses launched, but **only 14 benchmarked** by Skills England
- **60% of courses are paid**, even some marked as "free" — creating a deceptive funnel
- Courses found that are **10+ years old**, some over 20 years old
- Broken links, courses that don't exist, courses requiring attendance in rural Canada
- A Microsoft "course" that simply links to a product manual
- A supposed University of Edinburgh course not taught by the university
- Some courses from "degree mills" with reviews calling them scams or AI-generated slop

### The Copyright Law Scandal
- A course on AI and copyright was removed after it was found to teach US "fair use" doctrine — which doesn't exist in UK law
- The course creator (VinciWorks) said they had **no contractual arrangement** with the Hub and were not consulted before their materials were posted
- Ed Newton-Rex called it "laughably shocking" that the government didn't have the right to use the IP in the first place

### Vendor Lock-In / US Dependency
- All 14 badged courses are from US big tech companies
- Courses teach vendor-specific ecosystems (Google teaches Gemini, Microsoft teaches Copilot, etc.) rather than platform-agnostic principles
- Computer Weekly described it as increasing dependency on US big tech — "the opposite of positioning the UK as an AI maker, not an AI taker"
- Contrasted unfavourably with France's move to phase out US big tech dependency

### User Experience Failures
- Real users reported free courses disappearing after completing pathways, replaced by paid Python/Azure certification content
- User feedback: "The website feels messy. I'm an everyday person. I don't want to be a programmer. I just want to understand how to use AI."
- "Beginner" pathways include advanced concepts like computational thinking and neural network architecture
- No sandbox/playground for learning by doing
- No community features, no ongoing support
- One reviewer was directed to a £750 AI Filmmakers masterclass and a £590 AI Advertising course

### Structural Criticisms
- Hub trains individuals but ignores organisational readiness
- No learning science behind the design (5-15% completion rates for self-paced online vs 70-90% for instructor-led)
- PwC awarded the contract over proven national AI skills providers — critics say this needs investigating
- Ed Newton-Rex: "The AI Skills Hub seems mostly to consist of rehashed sales propaganda written by big tech and low-quality slide decks meant for other countries"
- Conservatives called it "embarrassing" and "wasting taxpayers' money"

### Sources of Criticism
- The Telegraph (broke original story)
- Computer Weekly (digital sovereignty angle)
- Techosaurus (practitioner-led user testing — 5 critical flaws)
- Charting Gen AI (copyright law scandal)
- Medium (£4.1m for a link list)
- Open letter from academics and AI literacy organisations
- "AI Shills Hub" parody website launched (aishillshub.org.uk)

## 3. GWTH.ai's Opportunity

### Why GWTH.ai Is Perfectly Positioned
The criticisms map almost exactly to what GWTH.ai does differently:
- **Platform-agnostic, practical skills** — not vendor onboarding
- **UK-focused content** — not repurposed American courses
- **Beginner-friendly without being patronising** — not jumping to neural networks
- **Actually current** — not decade-old material
- **Learning by doing** — not just slide decks with a badge
- **Independent** — not a big tech sales funnel

### GWTH.ai Pricing (Revised Feb 2026)
- **Free labs** — standalone, genuinely free, no strings
- **Paid course** — £29/month for first 3 months, then £5/month ongoing
- Previously priced in USD ($37.50/month for 3 months then $7.50) — localised to GBP

## 5. The Pitch Strategy

### Lead with the Free Labs, Not the Paid Course
The biggest criticism of the Hub is free courses that turn into paid upsells. GWTH.ai must avoid being lumped in with this.

**For the Hub listing:** Pitch the free labs only. They're genuinely free, no vendor lock-in, no upsell bait-and-switch, platform-agnostic, UK-focused. This is what PwC needs to counter the "big tech sales funnel" narrative.

**The paid course sits behind as a natural next step.** Someone completes the labs, wants more depth — GWTH.ai offers a structured course at £29/month. This is transparent and honest, fundamentally different from what's being criticised.

### The Pitch to PwC
Frame as solving their problem, not asking for a favour:

> "I run GWTH.ai, a UK-based AI education platform specifically for beginners. I've seen the coverage about the Hub and I think I can help. My courses are platform-agnostic, UK-focused, built for non-technical people, and genuinely free. That's exactly what the critics are saying is missing. I'd love to explore how we could work together."

### For Skills England Benchmark Alignment
Position GWTH.ai content to align with the Skills England AI foundation skills benchmark:
- Understanding risks and consequences of using AI
- Creating prompts and adapting settings for generative AI tools
- Responsible and ethical AI use
- Practical workplace application

## 6. TechLocal Funding Opportunity

### What It Is
£27 million funding as part of the £187 million TechFirst programme. Available to UK-registered businesses and learning providers through two competitions:
1. Fund UK education providers to develop innovative AI degrees and graduate traineeships
2. Get more people into tech jobs in local communities

### Why This Matters for GWTH.ai
- Could bid for funding that **subsidises or fully covers** the GWTH.ai course cost for target learners
- Model: "GWTH.ai course, normally £29/month, fully funded through TechLocal for learners in [region]"
- David gets paid, learners get it free at point of use — best of both worlds
- The £29/month price point is low enough for government subsidy to cover entirely, high enough to signal quality

### Action Required
- Research TechLocal application process and deadlines
- Determine eligibility criteria for GWTH.ai
- Potentially combine with PwC connection (if PwC administers the applications)
